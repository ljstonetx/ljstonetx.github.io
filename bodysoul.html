Origins: The Elks Lodge Era (1922‚Äì1930s)

In 1922, the Mercedes Elks Lodge established the town‚Äôs first hospital‚Äîa small facility to serve the growing community. By 1928, they replaced it with a larger building, shown in the historic postcard. Featuring white stucco walls, red tile roofing, and soft arches, the structure embodied the popular Spanish Colonial Revival style of the era.

Although it lacked air conditioning, the hospital used open windows and a shaded front porch to offer some relief from the South Texas heat. Several potted plants decorated the entry steps. In the background of the image, the First Baptist Church is visible. [38]

Features and Transition to Public Ownership

The hospital included:

  • 12 private rooms
  • An open ward
  • A red tile roof and arched colonnade
  • A front porch typical of early 20th-century designs

Many local children were born there. However, during the economic hardship of the Great Depression, the Elks Lodge could no longer sustain operations. In the 1930s, the City of Mercedes took over, renaming it Mercedes General Hospital. [66]

Later Use and Closure

The hospital served Mercedes for several decades. In the early 1960s, it closed following the opening of Knapp Memorial Hospital in nearby Weslaco. The building then served as a nursing home before being demolished in the late 1960s to make way for a senior apartment high-rise. [66]

First Methodist Church / El Buen Pastor Iglesia Methodista

The First Methodist Church of Mercedes constructed a Mission Revival-style building in the 1930s. Characterized by arched windows and a stucco exterior, it served the congregation until the 1960s.

After the Methodists relocated, the building was used by El Buen Pastor Iglesia Methodista until the late 1960s, when it was demolished and rebuilt on the same site. [27]

Immanuel Lutheran Church

This Lutheran church, marked by the presence of palm trees at its entrance, was built in 1911. Its box-shaped steeple and simple design resemble rural German churches in Texas. According to postcard notes, the structure was later moved to San Juan, Texas. [17][59]

First Presbyterian Church of Mercedes

Founded in 1909 by Evangelist S.M. Glasgow, the First Presbyterian Church was built in 1912 at Fourth and Missouri. The sanctuary featured a diagonal interior layout, stucco-covered brick, and served its congregation for decades.

A 1914 newspaper noted:

‚ÄúPreaching services every Sabbath, except the 3rd, at 11 a.m. and 8 p.m. Sabbath school, 9:45 every Sabbath. Christian Endeavor, 7 p.m.‚Äù

In 1947, the church was remodeled with classrooms, a kitchen, and a Mission Revival-style parapet. A pipe organ imported from Germany, funded by the Mary Stotler Fund, was installed. When a new church was built on Texas Avenue in 1972, the organ was transferred and remains in use today. [48][60]

First Baptist Church of Mercedes

Organized in 1907, the First Baptist Church built its impressive complex in 1920, including:

  • Sanctuary and bell tower
  • Auditorium with balcony seating for 750
  • Classrooms, kitchen, and fellowship hall
  • Art glass windows and decorative electric lighting
